In 1895, the FA had found a new permanent home for the FA Cup Final at the site of the famous Crystal Palace Exhibition building. Years later the owners of the exhibition, who relied on tourism for their income, looked for a new attraction for the venue. They decided to form their own football team to play at the Palace Stadium.

There had been an amateur Crystal Palace team since 1861, formed by workers from the exhibition building, however the club had disappeared from historical records around 1876. The owners of the venue wanted a professional club to play at the Palace Stadium and gain some new income from the team.

Crystal Palace Football Club, originally nicknamed "The Glaziers", was formed on 10 September 1905 under the assistance of Edmund Goodman Aston Villa's assistant secretary. The club applied to enter the Football League along with Chelsea and Southampton, however they were unsuccessful, the only one of the three clubs to be denided entry to the Football League.

The club found itself in the Southern League Second Division for the 1905–06 season. The club was successful in its first season and was promoted to the First Division, being crowned as champions. they also during their first season played in the mid-week United Counties League, finishing runner-up to Watford, and it was in this competition that the club played their first match, winning 3–0 away to New Brompton.

The club remained in the Southern League until 1914, their one highlight the 1907 shock First Round victory over Newcastle United in the FA Cup. The outbreak of World War I led to the Navy taking ownership of The Crystal Palace and the club was forced to move to West Norwood F.C.'s ground at Herne Hill Velodrome.

Three years later the club moved again to The Nest due to Croydon Common F.C. folding. The club joined the Football League Third Division in the 1920–21 season, being crowned as champions and gaining promotion to the Second Division. During this time between 1913 and 1921, Palace also won the London Challenge Cup three times in 1913, 1914, and 1921. In 1924 Palace moved to a new stadium Selhurst Park, which is current stadium of the club today.
